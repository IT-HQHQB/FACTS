🏅 KG Reviewer

The KG Reviewer is the third independent evaluator in the FACTS approval chain. By the time a case reaches KG Review, it has already been cleared by the Welfare Reviewer and the Zone Inspector (ZI). The KG Reviewer conducts a senior-level final check on the counseling form, attachments, and all prior reviewer comments before either approving the case to advance to Operations Lead, sending it back for rework, or rejecting it outright.

KG Reviewer

The KG Reviewer receives cases that have already been cleared by both the Welfare Reviewer and the Zone Inspector (ZI). By this stage, two independent evaluators have verified the case. The KG Reviewer conducts a senior-level review — cross-checking the Counseling Form, attachments, and all prior reviewer comments — before making one of three decisions: Approve (forward to Operations Lead), Request Rework (return to DCM with comments), or Reject (close the case with documented reasons). This document covers 9 steps across the full KG Reviewer workflow.

Section A — Login & Dashboard
01
Login & Landing on the KG Reviewer Dashboard

The KG Reviewer accesses FACTS through the browser using their assigned credentials. After login, the system loads the KG Reviewer dashboard — a focused view showing all cases currently at the KG Review stage within the KG Reviewer's scope.

  • Open browser → navigate to the FACTS URL → enter Email and Password → click Login
  • On successful login, the KG Reviewer Dashboard loads showing key metrics for the reviewer's scope
  • Dashboard stat cards: Cases Awaiting KG Review, Cases Approved This Month, Cases Sent for Rework, Cases Rejected
  • A Recent Cases list at the bottom shows the most recently updated cases — sorted by SLA urgency by default
  • Left sidebar shows the available modules: Dashboard and Cases
  • The bell icon in the top bar shows unread notifications — cases approved by the ZI trigger automatic notifications to the KG Reviewer
  • The KG Reviewer's name, role label (KG Reviewer), and scope are shown in the top-right profile area
02
Navigating the Cases Module — Finding Cases to Review

All cases awaiting KG Review are visible in the Cases module. The KG Reviewer filters to see only cases at the KG Review stage and opens them one by one. Every case in this queue has already been cleared by both the Welfare Reviewer and the ZI.

  • Click Cases in the left sidebar → the cases list loads scoped to the KG Reviewer's scope
  • Filter by Status → "KG Review" to see only cases awaiting the KG Reviewer's action — this is the default filter on load for the KG Reviewer role
  • Each case row shows: Case Number (BS-XXXX / SHND-XXXX), Applicant Name, Case Type, SLA badge, Assigned DCM, Welfare Reviewer name, ZI name, and Date Reached KG Review
  • SLA badge colours: On Track Due Soon Breached — cases approaching or past SLA are highlighted prominently
  • Cases can be sorted by SLA urgency (oldest first) to manage the review queue
  • Click any case row → opens the Case Detail View
Context Every case in the KG Review queue has passed both Welfare Review and ZI Approval. Two independent reviewers have already evaluated the case and found it credible. The KG Reviewer is the third — and final — field-level evaluator before the case moves to Operations Lead.

04
Understanding the Case — Status History & Prior Reviewer Findings

Before opening the Counseling Form, the KG Reviewer reads the Status History and Comments tabs to understand the complete history of the case, including findings and rationale documented by the Welfare Reviewer and ZI.

  • Click Status History → review the full audit trail: case creation, counseling form submission, Welfare Review approval, ZI approval, and the date the case reached KG Review
  • If there were any prior Rework cycles, these are visible in the Status History — understand what issues were raised at each stage and how the DCM addressed them
  • Click Comments → read all comments posted to date, including the Welfare Reviewer's approval note and the ZI's approval comment
  • Pay close attention to any reservations or caveats either reviewer mentioned — even if they still approved, flagged concerns are relevant for the KG Reviewer's own assessment
  • Note any DCM comments that provide context beyond what is in the form itself
  • If both prior reviewers gave strong, well-reasoned approvals with no caveats, this is a positive signal — but does not replace the KG Reviewer's own judgment
05
Reading the Counseling Form — All 7 Sections

Click the Counseling Form tab to review the full form. The KG Reviewer focuses on the overall integrity and coherence of the case — verifying that figures are internally consistent and that the business case and financial projections hold up at a senior level of scrutiny.

KG Reviewer Focus Areas Per Section
Section KG Reviewer Focus
Section 1 — Personal Details Identity and Jamaat details confirmed; applicant's eligibility for Baaseteen / SHND within the KG's scope
Section 2 — Family Details Overall financial picture coherence — income, expenses, surplus/deficit, and declared assets all consistent with the assistance being requested
Section 3 — Business Assessment Counselor's objectivity and depth of assessment; business viability beyond surface-level description; track record or credibility of the proposed venture
Section 4 — Financial Assistance Total assistance quantum appropriateness; Enayat (grant) vs. Qardan (loan) split is justifiable; repayment terms realistic given Section 2 surplus
Section 5 — Economic Projections Year-by-year projection credibility; growth assumptions are grounded in the business context described in Section 3; Cash Surplus from Year 1 sufficient for Qardan repayment schedule
Section 6 — Action Plan & Mentors Action plan is specific, time-bound, and grounded in reality; mentor names are appropriate and qualified for the business type
Section 7 — Declaration All three signatures (Applicant, Counselor, TR Committee) present with valid and sequential dates
06
Checking Attachments & Supporting Documents

Click the Attachments tab to verify all uploaded documents. The KG Reviewer confirms that mandatory documents are present and consistent with the case data, and takes note of any optional documents that strengthen or raise questions about the case.

  • Mandatory: Business photograph, Quotation for Qardan end-use, Product brochure — all three must be present with a credible Uploaded status
  • Verify the Quotation amount matches the Qardan figure in Section 4 — discrepancies at this stage after two prior reviews are a serious concern
  • For optional documents (ITR, Financial Statements, Cancelled Cheque, PAN/Aadhar) — if present, cross-check declared income and asset figures against Section 2 data
  • The KG Reviewer has read-only access — cannot upload or remove documents. Any attachment issues must be raised in Comments and resolved via Rework

Section C — Decision & Outcome
07
Adding KG Review Comments

Before submitting a decision, the KG Reviewer documents their findings in the Comments tab. This comment is particularly significant — it is the last field-level review comment before the case advances to Operations Lead and eventually to the Executive Approval chain. It carries senior-level weight in the audit trail.

How to Add a Comment
  • Click the Comments tab → scroll through the full thread to read all prior notes — DCM, Welfare Reviewer, and ZI comments are all visible
  • Scroll to the bottom → type in the comment text area → click Post Comment
  • The comment is attributed to the KG Reviewer's name and role with a timestamp automatically
  • Multiple comments can be posted — the KG Reviewer can separate observations by topic if needed
  • Comments are permanent and visible to all downstream reviewers including Operations Lead and Executive Approval
What the KG Reviewer Should Write — by Decision Type
Decision Recommended Comment Content
Approving Confirm the KG Reviewer's independent evaluation and key conclusions. E.g., "Reviewed the full case including Counseling Form (all 7 sections), attachments, and prior reviewer comments. Financial picture is internally consistent. Business viability is credible given the applicant's background and declared assets. Enayat/Qardan split is appropriate. Economic projections are reasonable. Recommending for Operations Lead review."
Rework List specific numbered items. E.g., "Despite prior reviewer approvals, the following require correction before this case can proceed: 1. Section 5 Cash Surplus from Year 1 (₹3,200/month) is insufficient to service the proposed Qardan repayment (₹4,500/month). The projection or the Qardan amount must be revised. 2. Mentor in Section 6 is listed without any contact details or qualification notes. Please update."
Rejecting State the grounds clearly and specifically. E.g., "On senior review, the declared business gross revenue of ₹8,000/month is inconsistent with the applicant's claimed business experience of 5 years in the same field (Section 3). Additionally, the ITR filed shows income of ₹4.2L p.a., which does not align with the declared monthly income of ₹18,000. The discrepancies are material and the case does not merit further processing."
KG Comment Is Read by Operations Lead & Executive Approvers The KG Reviewer's comment is the most senior field-level note in the case. Operations Lead and Executive Approvers read this comment to quickly understand the quality and basis of the case. A thorough, well-reasoned KG approval comment significantly speeds up downstream processing.
Rework & Rejection Require a Comment First The system requires at least one comment to be posted before allowing a Rework or Rejection action. For a KG-level Rework, issues must be specific and documented — prior reviewer approvals must be acknowledged in the comment when overriding them with a Rework request.

08
Making the Decision — Approve, Rework, or Reject

After reviewing all case materials and posting a comment, the KG Reviewer takes a formal action using the workflow action buttons on the Case Detail page. There are three possible decisions available to the KG Reviewer.

The Three Decision Options
✅ Approve

The case has passed the KG Reviewer's senior-level evaluation. Clicking Approve moves the case to the Operations Lead stage. The Operations Lead is notified automatically. The KG approval is the final field-level clearance before the cover letter generation process begins.

🔁 Request Rework

The case has fixable issues not resolved in earlier reviews. Clicking Request Rework sends the case back to the DCM with status Rework Required. After corrections and resubmission, the case re-enters the review chain from Welfare Review before working its way back up to KG Review.

❌ Reject

The case does not meet criteria and should not proceed further. Clicking Reject closes the case permanently. The DCM and all assigned stakeholders are notified. The KG Reviewer's rejection comment becomes the official rejection reason on the case record.

Confirmation Dialog All three actions require confirmation before executing. A modal appears summarising the outcome — click Confirm to proceed or Cancel to return to the case and review further before deciding.
Step-by-Step: Submitting the Decision
  • Confirm a comment has been posted documenting the KG Reviewer's findings
  • Click the appropriate action button — Approve, Request Rework, or Reject — in the workflow action panel on the case detail page
  • Read the confirmation dialog carefully → click Confirm to execute the action
  • The system updates the case status, sends notifications to the next relevant party, and logs the KG Reviewer's action in Status History with a timestamp
  • The case is removed from the KG Reviewer's active queue and the Cases list is refreshed
KG Decision — Outcome Summary
Decision Case Status After Who Is Notified Next Step
Approve Operations Lead Operations Lead Operations Lead reviews; if approved, Cover Letter is generated
Request Rework Rework Required DCM DCM corrects → resubmits → Welfare Review → ZI → KG again
Reject Rejected / Closed DCM + Stakeholders Case closed; KG rejection reason permanently recorded
Rework Routes Back Through the Full Review Chain When the KG Reviewer sends a case for Rework, the resubmitted case re-enters the chain at Welfare Review — not directly back to KG. The case must clear Welfare Review and ZI Approval again before returning to the KG Reviewer. This ensures every resubmission is re-verified independently at each level.

What Happens After the KG Decision

Once the KG Reviewer submits their decision, FACTS automatically routes the case to the correct next stage and notifies all relevant parties. The KG Reviewer's active role in this case is now complete unless it returns following a rework cycle.

After Approval — Case Moves to Operations Lead
  • The case workflow advances to Operations Lead — the next stage in the approval chain
  • The Operations Lead receives an in-app notification to review the case
  • If the Operations Lead approves, the system triggers Cover Letter Generation — the auto-generated case summary document that will be used throughout the Executive Approval chain
  • The KG Reviewer can still view the case and its downstream progress through the Cases module, but has no further actions to take

After Rework Request — What the KG Reviewer Should Expect
  • The case status changes to Rework Required — the DCM is notified to make corrections to the Counseling Form
  • After the DCM resubmits, the case re-enters the review chain at Welfare Review — it must pass both Welfare Review and ZI Approval before returning to KG Review
  • The KG Reviewer is notified when the case arrives back at KG Review after clearing the earlier stages
  • On re-review, check the Comments thread for the DCM's response and confirm that every item raised in the rework request has been addressed before approving
After Rejection — Case is Closed
  • The case status changes to Rejected — the case is permanently closed; no further workflow actions are possible
  • The KG Reviewer's rejection comment is stored on the case record as the official rejection reason
  • The DCM and applicant are notified; the DCM can view the case and comments for reference and record-keeping
  • A rejected case can only be re-opened by an Admin or Super Admin if a formally justified basis for reconsideration is provided
