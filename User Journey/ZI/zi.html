🏛️ ZI — Zone Inspector

The Zone Inspector (ZI) is the second independent evaluator in the FACTS approval chain, receiving cases that have already been cleared by the Welfare Reviewer. The ZI reviews the full Counseling Form and supporting documents, considers the Welfare Reviewer's findings, and either approves the case to advance to KG Review, sends it back for rework, or rejects it. The ZI's approval represents the first zone-level endorsement of the case.

ZI — Zone Inspector

The Zone Inspector (ZI) receives cases that have already passed the Welfare Review stage. By the time a case reaches the ZI, the Welfare Reviewer has confirmed that the counseling form is complete, credible, and meets the basic welfare criteria. The ZI conducts an independent second-level evaluation — reviewing the Counseling Form, attachments, and all prior reviewer comments — before making one of three decisions: Approve (forward to KG Review), Request Rework (return to DCM with comments), or Reject (close the case with documented reasons). This document covers 9 steps across the full ZI workflow.

Section A — Login & Dashboard
01
Login & Landing on the ZI Dashboard

The Zone Inspector accesses FACTS through the browser using their assigned credentials. After login, the system loads the ZI dashboard — a focused view showing all cases that are currently at the ZI Approval stage within the ZI's zone scope.

  • Open browser → navigate to the FACTS URL → enter Email and Password → click Login
  • On successful login, the ZI Dashboard loads showing key metrics scoped to the ZI's zone
  • Dashboard stat cards: Cases Awaiting ZI Approval, Cases Approved This Month, Cases Sent for Rework, Cases Rejected
  • A Recent Cases list shows the most recently updated cases in the ZI's queue — sorted by SLA urgency by default
  • The left sidebar shows the ZI's available modules: Dashboard and Cases
  • The bell icon in the top bar shows unread notifications — new cases reaching ZI Approval stage trigger automatic notifications to the ZI
  • The ZI's name, role label (Zone Inspector), and zone scope are visible in the top-right profile area
02
Navigating the Cases Module — Finding Cases to Review

All cases awaiting ZI review are visible in the Cases module. The ZI filters to see only cases at the ZI Approval stage and opens them one by one. Every case in this queue has already been cleared by the Welfare Reviewer.

  • Click Cases in the left sidebar → the cases list loads, scoped to the ZI's zone
  • Filter by Status → "ZI Approval" to see only cases awaiting the ZI's action — this is the default filter on load for the ZI role
  • Each case row shows: Case Number (BS-XXXX / SHND-XXXX), Applicant Name, Case Type, SLA badge, Assigned DCM, Welfare Reviewer Name, and Date Reached ZI Approval
  • SLA badge colours: On Track Due Soon Breached — cases close to or past SLA are highlighted prominently
  • Cases can be sorted by SLA (oldest first) or by Case Number to manage the review queue systematically
  • Click any case row → opens the Case Detail View for that case
Context Every case in the ZI Approval queue has already passed Welfare Review. The Welfare Reviewer's approval comment is on record. The ZI is not the first reviewer — they are the second level of independent evaluation.

Case Detail View — Header, Workflow Position & Tabs

Clicking a case opens the Case Detail View. The page shows a case summary header, the current workflow position highlighted on the progress bar, and a tab strip. The ZI will use the Counseling Form, Attachments, Comments, and Status History tabs.

Case Header Information
  • Case Number (e.g., BS-0042), Applicant Name, Case Type badge, and current Workflow Stage: ZI Approval badge shown prominently
  • SLA Timer shows the ZI-stage deadline — days remaining or days overdue for the ZI to complete this review
  • Assigned DCM name, Welfare Reviewer name, and the date the case entered ZI Approval are all displayed
  • Workflow Progress Bar shows all stages; Welfare Review is shown as completed ✓ and ZI Approval is the active stage

04
Understanding the Case — Status History & Welfare Review Findings

Before opening the Counseling Form, the ZI reads the Status History and Comments tabs to understand the full case history — including the Welfare Reviewer's findings and whether this is a first-time ZI review or a resubmission after rework.

  • Click the Status History tab → review the complete audit trail: case creation, counseling form completion, Welfare Review approval, and the date the case was forwarded to ZI Approval
  • If the case was previously sent for Rework at any earlier stage, the Status History will show those cycles — understand what issues were raised and how the DCM resolved them
  • Click the Comments tab → read all comments posted on the case so far, starting from the bottom (most recent)
  • The Welfare Reviewer's approval comment is visible here — read it carefully to understand what they reviewed and what they specifically found satisfactory
  • If the Welfare Reviewer raised concerns but still approved, note those concerns — they may be relevant to the ZI's own assessment
  • Note whether there are any DCM comments providing context about the applicant's situation beyond what's in the form
05
Reading the Counseling Form — All 7 Sections

Click the Counseling Form tab to read the full form in read-only mode. The ZI evaluates the case independently — not just checking that the Welfare Reviewer's points are addressed, but forming their own view on the case's completeness, credibility, and merit.

ZI Focus Areas in Each Section
Section Key ZI Focus Points
Section 1 — Personal Details Identity confirmed, Jamaat within ZI's zone scope, no discrepancies in basic information
Section 2 — Family Details Declared family income vs. assets — overall financial picture consistency; Surplus/Deficit figure and its implication for repayment
Section 3 — Business Assessment Counselor's assessment quality and objectivity; proposed business suitability for the applicant's background; market realism
Section 4 — Financial Assistance Enayat vs. Qardan split appropriateness; total assistance amount proportionality; repayment feasibility given Section 2 surplus
Section 5 — Economic Projections Year-over-year growth rate realism; Cash Surplus sufficient to service Qardan repayments; overall financial trajectory credibility
Section 6 — Action Plan & Mentors Specificity and achievability of the action plan; mentor assignments appropriate and qualified for the business type
Section 7 — Declaration All three signatures present (Applicant, Counselor, TR Committee) with valid dates; no anomalies in declaration section
06
Checking Attachments & Supporting Documents

Click the Attachments tab to verify all supporting documents. The ZI checks that mandatory documents are present and that their content is consistent with the Counseling Form data.

  • Mandatory documents: Business photograph, Quotation for Qardan end-use, Product brochure — all three must be present and credible
  • Verify the Quotation amount aligns with the Qardan amount in Section 4 — a significant mismatch is a red flag
  • The business photograph should show a real, credible location matching the description in Section 3
  • If optional documents are present (ITR, Financial Statements, PAN, Aadhar) — cross-check them against the declared figures in Sections 2 and 3
  • The ZI has read-only access — cannot upload or remove documents. Issues with attachments must be documented in Comments and resolved via a Rework request

Section C — Decision & Outcome
07
Adding ZI Review Comments

Before submitting a decision, the ZI documents their observations and evaluation in the Comments tab. The ZI's comment becomes part of the permanent case audit trail and is visible to all subsequent reviewers in the chain — KG Review, Operations Lead, Executive Approval, and beyond.

How to Add a Comment
  • Click the Comments tab → scroll through the full comment thread to read all prior notes (DCM comments, Welfare Reviewer's approval note)
  • Scroll to the bottom → type in the comment text area → click Post Comment
  • The comment is timestamped and attributed to the ZI's name and role automatically
  • Multiple comments can be posted — the ZI can separate their review observations by topic if needed
  • Comments cannot be deleted — write with care and professionalism
What the ZI Should Write — by Decision Type
Decision Recommended Comment Content
Approving Confirm key strengths from the ZI's review. E.g., "Reviewed the full Counseling Form and attachments. Financial picture is consistent — declared income (₹X/month) aligns with the surplus figure. Business plan is grounded in the applicant's background. Quotation verified against Section 4 amounts. Recommending for KG Review."
Rework List specific, numbered corrections required. E.g., "1. Section 5 projection — Year 2 revenue jump of 3× over Year 1 needs justification given current business scale. 2. Business photograph shows a residential premise, not a commercial unit as described in Section 3. Please clarify and update. 3. TR Committee signature date precedes the applicant's signature date — needs correction."
Rejecting State the grounds clearly. E.g., "Applicant's declared assets (property ₹40L + vehicles ₹8L) combined with a monthly family income of ₹65,000 indicate the family does not meet the financial need threshold for Baaseteen assistance. Welfare criteria not satisfied. Case rejected."
Comments Are Visible to All Downstream Reviewers The ZI's comment is read by KG Review, Operations Lead, and the Executive Approval chain. A well-documented ZI approval comment that clearly articulates why the case is strong helps downstream reviewers process the case faster and with greater confidence.
Rework & Rejection Require a Comment First The system will not allow a Rework or Rejection action unless at least one comment has been posted on the case. This ensures every Rework and Rejection is backed by a documented reason visible to the DCM, the applicant record, and all reviewers.

08
Making the Decision — Approve, Rework, or Reject

After reviewing all case materials and posting a comment, the ZI takes a formal action using the workflow action buttons on the Case Detail page. There are three possible decisions available to the ZI.

The Three Decision Options
✅ Approve

The case meets the ZI's evaluation criteria. Clicking Approve moves the case to the KG Review stage. The KG reviewer assigned to this zone is notified automatically. The ZI's approval is recorded in the Status History.

🔁 Request Rework

The case has fixable issues. Clicking Request Rework sends the case back to the DCM with status Rework Required. The DCM is notified with the ZI's comments. After correction and resubmission, the case goes back through Welfare Review before returning to the ZI.

❌ Reject

The case does not meet criteria and should not proceed. Clicking Reject closes the case permanently. The DCM and all assigned stakeholders are notified. The ZI's rejection comment becomes the official rejection reason on record.

Confirmation Dialog All three actions require confirmation before executing. A modal appears: "Are you sure you want to [Action] this case?" with a summary of the outcome — click Confirm to proceed or Cancel to go back and review further.
Step-by-Step: Submitting the Decision
  • Confirm a comment has been posted summarising the ZI's review findings
  • Click the appropriate action button — Approve, Request Rework, or Reject — in the workflow action panel on the case detail page
  • Read the confirmation dialog carefully — it confirms what will happen next → click Confirm
  • The system executes the action, updates the case status, sends notifications to the relevant next parties, and logs the ZI's action in Status History with a timestamp
  • The case moves out of the ZI's active queue — the ZI is returned to the Cases list
ZI Decision — Outcome Summary
Decision Case Status After Who Is Notified Next Step in Workflow
Approve KG Review KG Reviewer KG Reviewer evaluates and approves or rejects
Request Rework Rework Required DCM DCM corrects form → resubmits → Welfare Review → ZI again
Reject Rejected / Closed DCM + Stakeholders Case closed; ZI's rejection reason permanently recorded
Rework Routes Back Through Welfare Review When the ZI sends a case for Rework, the DCM corrects the Counseling Form and resubmits. The case does not skip Welfare Review — it goes back through the full review chain starting at Welfare Review before reaching the ZI again. This ensures every resubmission is independently re-verified at each stage.

What Happens After the ZI Decision

Once the ZI submits their decision, FACTS automatically routes the case to the appropriate next stage and notifies all relevant parties. The ZI's role in this case is now complete unless the case later returns for re-review after a rework cycle.

After Approval — Case Moves to KG Review
  • The case workflow advances to KG Review — the next level in the approval chain
  • The KG Reviewer assigned to this zone receives an in-app notification to begin their evaluation
  • The ZI can still view the case and track its progress through the Cases module but has no further action to take
  • The case status badge in the Cases list updates to "KG Review"

After Rework Request — What the ZI Should Expect
  • The case status changes to Rework Required — the DCM receives a notification to correct the Counseling Form
  • Once the DCM resubmits, the case re-enters Welfare Review first — the Welfare Reviewer must re-approve before the case returns to the ZI
  • The ZI receives a new notification when the case arrives back at ZI Approval after re-clearing Welfare Review
  • On re-review, the ZI should check the Comments thread for the DCM's response to the rework request and confirm each raised point has been addressed before approving
After Rejection — Case is Closed
  • The case status changes to Rejected — closed permanently with no further workflow actions possible
  • The ZI's rejection comment is stored permanently as the official reason on the case record
  • The DCM and applicant are notified; the DCM can view the case and the ZI's comments for reference
  • A rejected case can only be re-opened by an Admin or Super Admin if there is a formally justified reason to reconsider
