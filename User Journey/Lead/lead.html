🎯 Operations Lead

The Operations Lead receives cases that have already been cleared by three prior independent reviewers — Welfare Reviewer, ZI (Zone Inspector), and KG Reviewer. The Operations Lead conducts their own senior-level evaluation of the case data: reviewing the Counseling Form, Attachments, Status History, and all prior reviewer comments. Based on this review, they make one of three decisions: Approve, Request Rework, or Reject. If approved, the system automatically triggers the Cover Letter Generation process — the case then moves forward to the Executive Approval chain.

01
Login & Landing on the Operations Lead Dashboard

The Operations Lead accesses FACTS via browser with their credentials. After login, the system loads a role-scoped dashboard surfacing all cases currently at the Operations Lead stage within their Jamiat scope.

  • Navigate to the FACTS URL → enter Email / Username + Password → click Login. JWT token issued and stored.
  • Dashboard stat cards (scoped to OL's Jamiat/Jamaat): Cases Awaiting My Review · Approved This Month · Sent for Rework · Rejected
  • A Recent Cases panel shows cases most recently forwarded from KG Review — sorted by SLA urgency
  • Left sidebar: Dashboard and Cases modules. Bell icon shows unread notifications — KG Reviewer approval triggers an automatic in-app notification to the Operations Lead.
  • The Operations Lead's name, role label, and Jamiat scope are displayed in the top-right profile area
02
Navigating the Cases Module — Finding Cases to Review

All cases awaiting Operations Lead action are in the Cases module. Every case in this queue has been cleared independently by three prior reviewers.

  • Click Cases in the left sidebar → cases list loads, pre-filtered to "Operations Lead" status for this role
  • Each row shows: Case Number (BS-XXXX / SHND-XXXX), Applicant Name, Case Type, SLA badge, DCM, Welfare Reviewer, ZI, KG Reviewer names, Date Reached OL
  • SLA badges: On Track Due Soon Breached — sort by SLA urgency to prioritise the queue
  • Click any row → opens the Case Detail View
Context Every case in the Operations Lead queue has already passed three prior independent reviews — Welfare Review, ZI Approval, and KG Review. The Operations Lead conducts their own senior-level independent evaluation. On approval, the system automatically triggers Cover Letter Generation — the case then advances to Executive Approval.

04
Reading Status History & All Prior Reviewer Comments

Before opening the Counseling Form, the Operations Lead reads the full history and comments thread to understand every prior evaluation — including findings, caveats, and rationale from all three prior reviewers.

  • Click Status History → audit trail: Case Created → Draft → Counseling Submitted → Welfare Approved → ZI Approved → KG Approved → Operations Lead, all timestamped
  • Check for any prior Rework cycles — how many, what issues were raised, and how they were resolved before reaching OL
  • Click Comments → full thread: DCM's submission note → Welfare Reviewer's approval rationale → ZI's independent findings → KG Reviewer's senior approval note
  • Any reservations flagged by the KG Reviewer (e.g., "projections appear slightly optimistic") are especially relevant — the OL is the last reviewer before Executive Approval and must ensure every concern has been adequately addressed
  • The Operations Lead is not bound by prior reviewer assessments but must document any divergence in their own comment before deciding
05
Reviewing the Counseling Form & Attachments

The Operations Lead reads the full Counseling Form with a focus on financial coherence and overall case integrity, then checks all attachments.

Key Focus Areas Per Section
SectionOperations Lead Focus
Section 1 — Personal DetailsITS number, Jamiat/Jamaat, age, occupation — confirm eligibility and scope
Section 2 — Family DetailsTotal family income, declared expenses, monthly surplus/deficit — is the declared financial position internally consistent? Existing liabilities (Qardan, goods credit, others)
Section 3 — Business AssessmentBusiness viability at a senior level; counselor's objectivity and depth; present vs. proposed business condition coherence
Section 4 — Financial AssistanceRequested Enayat and Qardan amounts; 5-year repayment schedule — are the amounts proportionate to the family's repayment capacity from Section 2?
Section 5 — Economic Growth5-year projections: Revenue, Expenses, Profit, Cash Surplus per year. Verify Year 1 Cash Surplus covers the proposed Qardan repayment. Are projection growth assumptions realistic?
Section 6 — Action Plan & MentorsSpecific, time-bound action plan; qualified mentors listed — is the upliftment strategy credible?
Section 7 — DeclarationAll three signatures present (Applicant, Counselor, TR Committee) with valid and sequential dates
Attachments Check
  • Click Attachments → verify all mandatory docs are present: Business photograph Uploaded, Quotation for Qardan end-use Uploaded, Product brochure Uploaded
  • Cross-check the Quotation amount against the requested Qardan in Section 4 — they should align
  • If optional documents (ITR, Financial Statements) are present, verify declared income figures match Section 2 data
  • The Operations Lead has read-only access — any attachment issues must be raised via Rework

06
Adding Comments & Making the Decision (Approve / Rework / Reject)

After reviewing all case materials, the Operations Lead posts a workflow comment documenting their findings and then uses the action panel to formally decide the case.

Posting a Comment
  • Click the Comments tab → read all prior comments → scroll to bottom → type comment → click Post Comment
  • The Operations Lead's comment is the last field-level review note before Executive Approval — it is read by all Executive Approvers and carries significant weight
  • For Rework and Reject actions, a comment is required before the action can be submitted
DecisionRecommended Comment Content
ApproveE.g., "Reviewed full case including Counseling Form (all 7 sections), attachments, and all prior reviewer comments. Financial position is consistent — monthly surplus supports the proposed Qardan repayment schedule. Business assessment is credible. Upliftment plan in Section 6 is specific and time-bound. Approving for Cover Letter Generation and Executive Review."
ReworkList numbered specific items: "1. Year 1 Cash Surplus (₹2,100/month) cannot support the proposed Qardan repayment (₹4,167/month over 36 months) — revise projections or reduce Qardan ask. 2. Quotation (₹90K) does not match Qardan request (₹1,50K) — clarify end-use split. 3. Action Plan in Section 6 lacks specific milestone dates."
RejectState clear material grounds: "Existing liabilities (goods credit ₹25K) combined with a monthly surplus of ₹1,400 leave no viable Qardan repayment capacity. Declared monthly income (₹12K) is materially inconsistent with the submitted ITR (₹37.5K/month). Discrepancies are not resolvable through rework — rejecting."
The Three Decision Options
✅ Approve

The case has passed the Operations Lead's evaluation. Clicking Approve is the final field-level clearance. The system immediately triggers Cover Letter Generation automatically — the Cover Letter PDF is compiled from the case data and the case advances to Executive Approval.

🔁 Request Rework

The case has fixable issues. Clicking Request Rework returns the case to the DCM with status Rework Required. After corrections and resubmission, the case must pass Welfare Review → ZI Approval → KG Review before returning to Operations Lead.

❌ Reject

The case does not merit further processing. Clicking Reject closes the case permanently. The DCM and all stakeholders are notified. The Operations Lead's rejection comment becomes the official rejection reason on the record.

What Happens After Approval — Cover Letter Generation & Next Steps

When the Operations Lead approves the case, FACTS immediately and automatically triggers the Cover Letter Generation process. The Operations Lead's active role in this case is now complete.

Immediate Automated Sequence on OL Approval
⚡
OL clicks Approve → System auto-triggers: (1) Cover Letter PDF is compiled from case data → (2) Cover Letter tab activates on the case → (3) Case status advances to Executive Approval → (4) Level 1 Executive Approvers receive in-app notification with the Cover Letter attached.
  • Cover Letter PDF Generated: The system compiles the applicant's case data — profile, family financial summary, business details, financial assistance figures, 5-year projections, and the review chain — into a formatted PDF document. A new record is created in the cover_letters table with file_path, generated_at, and generated_by (OL user ID).
  • Cover Letter tab activates: The Cover Letter tab on the case becomes accessible to the Operations Lead and all downstream roles. The tab shows a PDF preview with a Download button.
  • Case status advances: cases.current_workflow_stage_id is updated to the Cover Letter Generation → Executive Approval stage. A new entry is written to status_history with a timestamp.
  • Executive Approvers notified: All assigned Level 1 Executive Approvers receive an in-app notification referencing the case. The Cover Letter PDF is the primary document they review in the Executive Approval stage.

After Rework — What the Operations Lead Should Expect
  • The case returns to the DCM for corrections → after resubmission, it must pass Welfare Review → ZI Approval → KG Review before arriving back at Operations Lead
  • On re-arrival, check the Comments thread for the DCM's response — confirm every item raised in the rework comment has been addressed before approving
  • A second round of comments is recommended, noting what was verified on re-review
After Rejection — Case is Closed
  • The case status changes to Rejected / Closed — the case is permanently closed; no further workflow actions are possible
  • The Operations Lead's rejection comment is stored as the official rejection reason on the case record
  • The DCM and all assigned stakeholders are notified; the case can be viewed in read-only mode for records
  • A rejected case can only be re-opened by an Admin or Super Admin if a formally justified basis for reconsideration is provided
