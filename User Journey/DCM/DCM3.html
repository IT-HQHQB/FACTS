DCM — Deputy Counseling Manager

The Deputy Counseling Manager (DCM) works exclusively within the Cases module in FACTS. The DCM's job is to open each assigned case, navigate to the Counseling Form, and carefully fill all seven sections — capturing the applicant's personal background, family situation, financial data, business assessment, economic projections, and formal declarations. Once the form is complete, the DCM submits the case forward for the next level of approval. This document covers 12 steps across the full DCM workflow.

Section A — Login & Cases Module
01
Login & Landing on Dashboard

The DCM accesses FACTS via the web browser. After authenticating, the system loads the DCM dashboard — a quick summary of all cases under the DCM's scope.

  • Open browser → navigate to the FACTS URL → enter Email and Password → click Login
  • On successful login, the system loads the DCM Dashboard showing total assigned cases, cases in counseling, and cases needing action
  • The left sidebar shows the DCM's available modules: Dashboard and Cases
  • The bell icon in the top bar shows any unread notifications (new case assignments, rework alerts)
  • The DCM's name, role label, and Jamaat scope are visible in the top-right profile area
02
Opening the Cases Module & Finding Your Case

All of the DCM's work happens in the Cases module. From here, the DCM can view all cases within their Jamaat scope, filter by status, and open a specific case to begin filling the counseling form.

  • Click Cases in the left sidebar → the Cases list loads showing all cases scoped to the DCM's Jamaat
  • Search bar at the top allows searching by Case Number (BS-XXXX), Applicant Name, or ITS Number
  • Filter options: filter by Case Status (Draft, Counseling, Cover Letter Stage, etc.), Case Type, or SLA status
  • Each case row shows: Case Number, Applicant Name, Case Type, Current Stage, Assigned Counselor, SLA badge, and Last Updated date
  • Cases assigned to the DCM that are awaiting counseling form completion will show status In Counseling
  • Click on any case row → opens the Case Detail View

03
Case Detail View — Tabs & Overview

Clicking a case opens the Case Detail View — the central screen where the DCM does all their work. This page shows the case summary header, the current workflow stage, and a set of tabs. The most important tab for the DCM is the Counseling Form tab.

Case Header
  • Case Number (e.g., BS-0042), Applicant Name, Case Type, and current Workflow Stage badge are shown prominently at the top
  • SLA indicator shows time remaining or breach status for the current stage
  • Assigned Counselor name is shown; DCM can see who is responsible
  • A Workflow Progress Bar visualises all stages: Draft → Counseling → Cover Letter → Welfare Review → Approvals → Disbursement

Starting Point for the DCM Every time the DCM opens a case, they should click the Counseling Form tab first. The tab shows a section progress overview — each of the 7 sections shows its current status: Not Started, In Progress, or Complete. The DCM works through them one by one.

Section B — Counseling Form: Personal & Family
04
Counseling Form — Section 1: Personal Details

Click the Counseling Form tab on the case → the form opens showing all 7 sections as a progress list at the top. Click Section 1: Personal Details to begin. Many fields are auto-populated from the applicant's ITS profile — review and complete any missing ones.

Fields in Section 1 — Personal Details
  • ITS Number — auto-populated, read-only
  • Full Name — auto-populated from ITS
  • Age — enter or confirm from ITS
  • Education Level — dropdown (Primary / Secondary / Graduate / etc.)
  • Jamiat — auto-populated from ITS
  • Jamaat — auto-populated from ITS
  • Contact Number — verify or enter mobile number
  • Email Address — optional contact email
  • Residential Address — full home address
  • Present Occupation — current job or business type
  • Occupation Address — workplace or business location
  • Other Information — any additional personal notes
  • Click Save Section → section status changes to Complete in the progress tracker
  • You can return and edit any section before marking the whole form complete
05
Counseling Form — Section 2: Family Details (Wellbeing & Members)

Section 2 covers the applicant's family situation in detail. It is split into multiple sub-sections — Wellbeing, Family Members Table, Income & Expenses, and Assets & Liabilities. Start by filling the Wellbeing notes and the Family Members table.

Sub-section: Wellbeing Notes
  • Food: text area — describe the family's food security situation
  • Housing: text area — owned, rented, shared, or other housing condition
  • Education: text area — children's schooling status, any gaps or concerns
  • Health: text area — any chronic illness, disability, or medical expenses
  • Deeni: text area — religious participation and community standing
  • Ziyarat / Travel / Recreation: text area — travel or recreational capacity
Sub-section: Family Members Table
  • Click "Add Row" for each family member — fill: Name, Age, Relation (spouse/child/parent/etc.), Education Level, Occupation, Annual Income
  • Add as many rows as needed; rows can be deleted using the remove icon on the right
  • Total annual income across all earning members is auto-summed at the bottom of the table

06
Counseling Form — Section 2 cont'd: Income, Expenses, Assets & Liabilities

The lower half of Section 2 captures the family's financial picture — monthly and yearly income/expenses broken down by source, followed by a complete assets and liabilities snapshot. These numbers feed directly into the Cover Letter and financial assessment.

Sub-section: Income & Expenses Grid
  • The grid has rows for each income/expense source and two columns: Monthly and Yearly
  • Income rows: Business Income, Salary/Wages, Home Industry, Other Income
  • Expense rows: Food & Groceries, Rent/Housing, Utilities, Education Fees, Medical, Other Expenses
  • Enter monthly figures → yearly values auto-calculate (monthly × 12); or enter yearly directly
  • Total Income and Total Expenses rows auto-sum at the bottom
  • Surplus / Deficit is automatically computed: Total Income minus Total Expenses — shown with green (surplus) or red (deficit) highlight
Sub-section: Assets
  • Residential Property: estimated value of owned home
  • Shop / Commercial Land: value of any business premises owned
  • Machinery & Equipment: production or trade machinery
  • Vehicles: cars, motorcycles, commercial vehicles
  • Stock / Inventory: current goods held for business
  • Gold / Jewellery: estimated gold or jewellery value
  • Total Assets is auto-summed from all fields above
Sub-section: Liabilities
  • Qardan Hasana Borrowings: outstanding welfare loans taken
  • Goods / Trade Credit: credit owed to suppliers or traders
  • Other Liabilities: any other outstanding debts
  • Total Liabilities is auto-summed; Net Worth = Total Assets − Total Liabilities is displayed
  • Click Save Section → Section 2 marked Complete

Section C — Counseling Form: Business & Financial Assessment
07
Counseling Form — Section 3: Background Assessment & Proposed Business

Section 3 is the counselor's professional evaluation of the applicant. It is divided into two parts: the applicant's Background (education, experience, skills) and the Proposed Business details and market assessment. This is the most narrative-heavy section.

Sub-section A: Background
  • Education Background: describe highest education achieved and relevance to the proposed business
  • Work Experience: previous employment history, roles held, and duration
  • Family Business History: any prior family business activity or inherited trade skills
  • Skills & Knowledge: specific technical skills, trade knowledge, or vocational training
  • Counselor's Overall Assessment: free text — the DCM/counselor's holistic evaluation of the applicant's readiness and suitability for financial assistance
Sub-section B: Proposed Business Details
  • Present Business Condition: describe current state of the business
  • Trade Mark / Brand: existing brand name or logo if any
  • Online Presence: whether the business has social media, website, or digital channels
  • Digital Marketing: current or planned online marketing activities
  • Store / Unit Location: physical location or planned premises
  • Sourcing: where raw materials or stock are sourced from
  • Selling Channels: how goods/services are sold (retail, wholesale, online)
  • Major Expenses: primary cost items in the business
  • Goods Purchase Details: how inventory is purchased and at what frequency
  • Revenue Projections: estimated monthly or annual revenue
  • Profit Margin %: estimated gross margin on the business
Sub-section B: Products / Services Table
  • Click "Add Row" for each product or service offered
  • Columns: Product / Service Name, Unit of Measure, Cost Price, Selling Price
  • Margin per item is auto-calculated from cost vs. selling price
  • Click Save Section → Section 3 marked Complete

08
Counseling Form — Section 4: Financial Assistance Required

Section 4 captures exactly what type and amount of financial support the applicant is requesting, how they plan to repay it, and what non-financial support they also need.

Assistance Amount Fields
  • Total Amount Requested: overall sum sought
  • Enayat (Grant) Amount: non-repayable portion
  • Qardan Hasana Amount: interest-free loan portion
  • Self-Funding Contribution: amount applicant will add themselves
  • Rahen (Collateral): any collateral available
Support Needed (Multi-Select)
  • Industry Knowledge
  • Sourcing Support
  • Sales / Market Access
  • Internship Placement
  • Mentoring
  • Bookkeeping Training
Assistance Timeline Table
  • Click "Add Row" to define each disbursement phase
  • Columns: Purpose / Description, Enayat Amount, Qardan Amount, Repayment Duration (months)
  • Multiple rows can be added for phased disbursement plans (e.g., Phase 1 equipment, Phase 2 stock)
  • Click Save Section → Section 4 marked Complete
09
Counseling Form — Section 5: Economic Growth Projections

Section 5 captures the 5-year financial projection for the applicant's business. The DCM enters the expected figures year-by-year. Totals auto-calculate.

5-Year Projection Table — Columns
  • Rows: Last Year (Present) + Year 1, Year 2, Year 3, Year 4, Year 5
  • Revenue / Sales per year
  • Expenses breakdown: Raw Materials, Labour, Rent, Overhead, Repair & Maintenance
  • Total Expenses — auto-summed from breakdown rows
  • Gross Profit — auto-calculated: Revenue minus Total Expenses
  • Fund Allocation: Blocked Fund, Qardan Repayment amount, Other Income, Household Expenses
  • Cash Surplus — residual after all allocations; Additional Enayat and Qardan per year shown
Business Assets Projection Table
  • Separate table: asset values per year across — Cash, Raw Materials, Machines, Vehicles, Shop/Godown, Trademark
  • Click Save Section → Section 5 marked Complete

Counseling Form — Section 7: Declaration & Signatures

Section 7 is the final section of the Counseling Form. It collects formal declarations and digital signatures from three parties: the Applicant, the Counselor, and a TR Committee Member. All three signatures must be captured before the form can be marked complete.

Applicant Declaration
  • ITS Number — pre-filled from the case; read-only
  • Full Name — pre-filled from personal details
  • Contact Number — pre-filled; editable if needed
  • Declaration Date — date picker; defaults to today
  • Signature: two options — Upload Image (JPG/PNG of physical signature) or Draw on Canvas (on-screen signature pad using mouse or touch)
Counselor Declaration
  • Counselor ITS Number — enter the assigned counselor's ITS; auto-fetches name and contact
  • Declaration Date — date when counselor signs off
  • Signature: Upload or Draw options, same as applicant block
TR Committee Declaration
  • TR Committee Member ITS Number — enter their ITS; auto-fetches name and contact
  • Declaration Date — date of TR Committee signoff
  • Signature: Upload or Draw — same interface as above
  • Once all three signatures are captured, click Save Section → Section 7 marked Complete
All Three Signatures Are Mandatory The system will not allow the form to be marked complete unless all three declaration blocks have a signature recorded. Missing signatures show a red validation error on the section progress tracker.

Mark Form Complete & Submit for Approval

Once all 7 sections show Complete status, the DCM marks the counseling form as done and submits the case to the next level — the Welfare Review stage — where designated reviewers will evaluate the case for approval.

Step 1 — Review Section Completion Status
  • From the Counseling Form tab, the top of the page shows the 7-section progress tracker
  • Each section shows one of: Not Started In Progress Complete
  • All 7 must be Complete before proceeding — click any incomplete section to return and finish it
Step 2 — Mark Form Complete
  • Click "Mark Form Complete" button — system runs a final validation on all 7 sections
  • If any section has missing required fields, a validation error highlights the specific section and field
  • On successful validation: the form is locked — no further edits allowed unless rework is requested
  • The case workflow stage automatically advances to Cover Letter Stage

Closure
  • After the form is complete, the Cover Letter tab becomes active — a summary letter is auto-generated from the counseling form data for use by reviewers
  • The DCM can optionally review and submit the Cover Letter from the Cover Letter tab to formally push the case to Welfare Review
  • In-app notifications are sent to the Welfare Reviewer(s) assigned to the case's Jamiat, alerting them to begin their review
  • The case status updates to "Welfare Review" — visible in the Cases list and the case header
What Happens Next The DCM's job for this case is now complete. The case moves through: Counseling Form → Welfare Review → ZI Approval → KG Review → Operations Lead → Cover Letter Generation → Executive Approval (Multi-level) → Manzoori (Sanction) → Financial Disbursement → Payment Schedule Tracking → Fund Utilization (Quarterly) → Case Closure. If reviewers request changes at any stage, the case returns to the DCM as "Rework Required" — the DCM will receive a notification, unlock the relevant sections, make corrections, and resubmit.
