🔍 Welfare Reviewer

The Welfare Reviewer is the first independent evaluator in the FACTS approval chain. After the DCM completes and submits the Counseling Form, the case reaches Welfare Review. The Reviewer reads all case data, assesses eligibility and financial need, reviews the Counseling Form and supporting attachments, and either approves the case to move forward to ZI Approval, sends it back for rework, or rejects it with documented reasons.

Welfare Reviewer

The Welfare Reviewer receives cases once the DCM has completed the Counseling Form. The Reviewer's job is to independently evaluate the case — reading the full counseling form, checking attached documents, and then making one of three decisions: Approve (forward to ZI Approval), Request Rework (return to DCM with comments), or Reject (close the case with documented reasons). Cover Letter Generation happens later in the workflow, after Operations Lead approval. This document covers 9 steps across the full Welfare Reviewer workflow.

Section A — Login & Dashboard
01
Login & Landing on the Welfare Reviewer Dashboard

The Welfare Reviewer accesses FACTS through the browser using their assigned credentials. After login, the system loads the Welfare Reviewer dashboard — a focused view showing all cases currently awaiting review within their Jamiat scope.

  • Open browser → navigate to the FACTS URL → enter Email and Password → click Login
  • On successful login, the Welfare Reviewer Dashboard loads showing key metrics for the reviewer's Jamiat scope
  • Dashboard stat cards: Cases Awaiting My Review, Cases I Approved This Month, Cases Sent for Rework, Cases Rejected
  • A Recent Cases list shows the most recently updated cases in the reviewer's queue
  • Left sidebar shows modules available: Dashboard and Cases
  • Bell icon in the top bar shows unread notifications — new cases assigned for review trigger notifications automatically
02
Navigating the Cases Module — Finding Cases to Review

All cases awaiting welfare review are visible in the Cases module. The Reviewer filters to see only cases at the Welfare Review stage and opens them one by one to begin the review process.

  • Click Cases in the left sidebar → cases list loads scoped to the reviewer's Jamiat
  • Filter by Status → "Welfare Review" to see only cases awaiting the reviewer's action — this is the default filter on load for the Welfare Reviewer role
  • Each case row shows: Case Number (BS-XXXX), Applicant Name, Case Type, SLA badge, Assigned DCM, and Date Reached Welfare Review
  • SLA badge colours: On Track Due Soon Breached — cases close to or past SLA are highlighted prominently
  • Cases can be sorted by SLA (oldest first) to prioritise urgent reviews
  • Click any case row → opens the Case Detail View

03
Case Detail View — Header, Progress Bar & Tabs

Clicking a case opens the Case Detail View. This is where the Welfare Reviewer does all their evaluation work. The page shows a case summary header, the current workflow position, and a tab strip. The Reviewer will primarily use the Cover Letter, Counseling Form, Attachments, and Comments tabs.

Case Header Information
  • Case Number (e.g., BS-0042), Applicant Name, Case Type badge, and current Workflow Stage: Welfare Review badge shown prominently
  • SLA Timer shows the deadline for completing this review — days remaining or days overdue
  • Assigned DCM name and the date the case entered Welfare Review are displayed
  • Workflow Progress Bar visualises all stages with the current stage highlighted

04
Understanding the Case — Applicant Summary & Context

Before diving into the full Counseling Form, the Welfare Reviewer first gets a quick overview of the case from the case header and the Status History tab. This gives context on who the applicant is, what stage the case is at, and any prior activity on the case.

  • The case header at the top of the Case Detail View shows: Case Number, Applicant Name, ITS Number, Case Type (Baaseteen / SHND), Assigned DCM, and the date the case entered Welfare Review
  • The SLA timer shows how many days remain before the review deadline — prioritise cases with fewer days remaining
  • Click the Status History tab → review the full audit trail to see when the case was created, when the counseling form was completed, and whether this is a first-time review or a resubmission after rework
  • If the case was previously sent back for Rework, the Status History will show the original rework request and the DCM's resubmission — check whether previous concerns were addressed
  • Note any DCM comments already posted in the Comments tab for additional context before opening the Counseling Form
Note — No Cover Letter at This Stage Cover Letter Generation occurs later in the workflow — after Operations Lead approval. At the Welfare Review stage, the Reviewer evaluates the case directly from the Counseling Form and Attachments. There is no Cover Letter document available to read at this point.
05
Reading the Counseling Form — All 7 Sections

After reviewing the Cover Letter, click the Counseling Form tab to read the full form in detail. All 7 sections are presented in read-only mode. Evaluate completeness, accuracy, and overall case strength.

What to Review in Each Section
Section Key Things to Check
Section 1 — Personal Details ITS verification, contact info completeness, Jamiat/Jamaat match
Section 2 — Family Details Wellbeing accuracy, family member count vs. income declared, surplus/deficit reasonableness
Section 3 — Assessment Business background credibility, counselor's assessment quality, proposed business viability
Section 4 — Financial Assistance Amount requested vs. stated need, Enayat/Qardan split reasonableness, collateral declared
Section 5 — Economic Projections Year-1 revenue vs. present; growth rate realism; Cash Surplus adequacy for Qardan repayment
Section 6 — Action Plan & Mentors Action plan specificity and timeline realism; mentor assignments present and appropriate
Section 7 — Declaration All three signatures present (Applicant, Counselor, TR Committee); dates valid

06
Checking Attachments & Supporting Documents

The Attachments tab shows all documents uploaded by the DCM during the counseling form process. The Welfare Reviewer must verify that mandatory documents are present, view them, and assess whether they support the case data. Missing or inconsistent attachments are grounds for requesting rework.

What the Reviewer Sees in the Attachments Tab
  • A list of all uploaded files grouped by document type — Mandatory documents listed first, then Optional ones
  • Each file row shows: Document name, File name, Upload date, and a View / Download button
  • A section header shows the total attachment count (e.g., "5 documents attached")
  • Mandatory documents that are uploaded show Uploaded; missing ones show Missing
Mandatory Documents to Verify
Document What to Check
Photograph of business / proposed place Image is clear and shows a real location; matches description in Section 3 of the form
Quotation for Qardan end-use Quotation amount matches or aligns with the Qardan amount requested in Section 4
Product brochure / marketing material Confirms the business type and product claims made in Section 3
Optional Documents — Check if Present
  • Income Tax Return: confirms declared income levels; verify approximate alignment with Section 2 income figures
  • Financial Statements: P&L and Balance Sheet if an existing business — cross-check against Section 3 and Section 5 projections
  • Cancelled Cheque / PAN / Aadhar: identity verification documents — note their presence
  • If key optional documents are missing for an existing business (e.g., no financials), the reviewer may note this as a concern in Comments
Reviewer Cannot Upload or Delete Attachments The Welfare Reviewer has read-only access to Attachments. They can view and download files but cannot add new documents or remove existing ones. If documents are missing or incorrect, the reviewer sends the case back for Rework and lists the required corrections in the Comments section.

Section C — Decision & Outcome
07
Adding Review Comments

Before making a final decision, the Welfare Reviewer documents their observations, concerns, and evaluation notes in the Comments tab. This creates a clear audit trail and, in the case of Rework or Rejection, gives the DCM actionable feedback.

How to Use the Comments Tab
  • Click the Comments tab → a threaded comment feed loads showing all previous comments on this case
  • Scroll to the bottom → a text area is available to type a new comment
  • Type the review observation → click Post Comment → the comment is added with the reviewer's name, role, and timestamp
  • Comments are visible to all users with access to the case — write clearly and professionally
  • Multiple comments can be posted for different concerns (e.g., one for form issues, one for attachment issues)
What to Write in Review Comments
Scenario What to include in the comment
Case is strong — approving Brief summary of strengths: e.g., "Income figures consistent, business plan credible, all mandatory attachments present. Recommending for ZI review."
Sending for Rework Specific list of items to correct: e.g., "1. Section 4 Qardan amount does not match quotation. 2. Business photo missing context. 3. Year-1 revenue projection appears too high vs. present baseline."
Rejecting the case Clear documented reason: e.g., "Applicant income exceeds eligibility threshold. Declared assets indicate financial sufficiency. Case does not meet Baaseteen welfare criteria."
Comments Are Part of the Permanent Audit Trail All comments are stored permanently and cannot be deleted. They are visible across all workflow stages to all authorised users. Always write with the assumption that applicants, committee members, and management may read them.

08
Making the Decision — Approve, Rework, or Reject

After reviewing all case materials, the Welfare Reviewer takes a formal action using the workflow action buttons visible on the Case Detail page. There are three possible decisions.

The Three Decision Options
✅ Approve

The case is complete, credible, and meets welfare criteria. Clicking Approve moves the case to the ZI Approval stage. A notification is sent to the ZI assigned to this Jamiat.

🔁 Request Rework

The case has fixable issues. Clicking Request Rework sends the case back to the DCM with status Rework Required. The DCM is notified to address the reviewer's comments and resubmit.

❌ Reject

The case does not meet welfare criteria and should not proceed. Clicking Reject closes the case. The DCM and relevant stakeholders are notified. The reviewer's comments become the official rejection reason.

Confirmation Required All three actions show a confirmation dialog before executing. For Reject, the system requires at least one comment to be posted first — this ensures a documented reason always exists on record.
Step-by-Step: How to Submit the Decision
  • Ensure at least one comment has been posted summarising the review findings
  • Click the appropriate action button: Approve, Request Rework, or Reject — found in the workflow action panel on the case detail page
  • A confirmation dialog appears: confirm the action → click Confirm
  • The system executes the action, updates the case status, triggers notifications, and logs the action in Status History
  • The case moves out of the reviewer's queue — the reviewer is returned to the Cases list
Decision Outcome Summary
Decision Case Status After Who is Notified Next Step
Approve ZI Approval ZI (Zone Inspector) ZI reviews and makes their decision
Request Rework Rework Required DCM DCM corrects form, resubmits → returns to Welfare Review
Reject Rejected / Closed DCM + Applicant Case closed; rejection reason permanently recorded

09
What Happens After the Decision

Once the Welfare Reviewer submits their decision, FACTS automatically routes the case and notifies all relevant parties. The Reviewer's role in this case is now complete unless the case returns for a re-review after rework.

After Approval — Case Moves to ZI Approval
  • The case workflow advances to ZI Approval — the next level of the approval chain
  • The ZI (Zone Inspector) assigned to the Jamiat receives an in-app notification to begin their review
  • The Welfare Reviewer can still view the case and its progress but has no further action unless the ZI sends it back

After Rework Request — Case Returns to DCM
  • The case status changes to Rework Required — the DCM receives a notification with the reviewer's comments attached
  • The DCM unlocks the relevant sections of the Counseling Form, makes the requested corrections, and resubmits
  • Once resubmitted, the case returns to Welfare Review stage — the Welfare Reviewer receives a new notification to re-review
  • The reviewer goes through the same review process (Steps 03–08) again, focusing on whether the rework comments have been addressed
After Rejection — Case is Closed
  • The case status changes to Rejected — the case is closed and no further workflow actions are possible
  • The rejection reason (from the reviewer's Comments) is stored permanently against the case
  • The DCM and applicant are notified of the rejection; the DCM can view the case and the comments for reference
  • A rejected case can only be re-opened by an Admin or Super Admin if there is a valid reason to reconsider
